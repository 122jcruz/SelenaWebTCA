About Selena

Selena Quintanilla was born on April 16, 1971 to Abraham and Marcella Quintanilla. With her two siblings, Suzette and AB Quintanilla, and with the help of their father, they formed a band called Selena y Los Dinos. The group would play Tejano music and would welcome members such as Ricky Vela, Pete Astudillo, Joe Ojeda, and Chris Perez as they became more mainstream music. They would blend different style of music together such as cumbia and tejano. Selena would eventually become known as La Reina De La Cumbia and La Reina de Tejano. She would go on to win various awards and 2 Grammys. Selena passed on March 31, 1995.

Selena's Legacy

Selena continues to be an inspirtation to all those who follow her. Her music is heard almost everywhere. Selena continues to be a style icon with her inovative fashion styles that were ahead if her time. Her legacy continues to be honored with the 1997 release of Selena The Movie, Selena the Series Netflix Version, To Selena with Love the novel. Selena was honored with the Lifetime Achievement Award at the 2021 Grammys.
